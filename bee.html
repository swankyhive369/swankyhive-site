Bee Knowledge

Discover the fascinating world of bees and learn why they're essential for our planet's ecosystem.

Why Bees Matter

Bees are vital pollinators responsible for a large portion of the food we eat. Many flowering plants and crops depend on bees and other pollinators to produce fruits and seeds, helping maintain biodiversity and feeding communities around the globe.

1

Five Amazing Eyes

Honeybees have two large compound eyes made of thousands of tiny lenses for color vision and three simple eyes called ocelli on top of their heads that detect light intensity.

2

The Waggle Dance

Bees communicate by performing dances and releasing pheromones. The famous waggle dance tells nest mates the direction and distance to nectar and pollen sources.

3

Busy Producers

A single honeybee produces only about one‑twelfth of a teaspoon of honey in its lifetime, but together a colony can make over 100 pounds of honey each year.

How You Can Help

A

Plant Bee‑Friendly Gardens

Create a haven for bees by planting native flowers and herbs that provide nectar and pollen throughout the growing season. Avoid using pesticides that harm pollinators.

B

Support Beekeepers

Buy local honey and beeswax products to support beekeepers who maintain healthy bee populations. Consider joining or donating to local conservation efforts.

C

Create Bee Habitats

Leave areas of your garden unmowed or provide nesting sites for solitary bees. You can even build or buy bee hotels for native bees.
